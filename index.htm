About

My background in Psychology (with an emphasis in Cognition) has led me to explore my interests in human cognition and preferences surrounding AI use. Guiding my work is a quasi-obsession with epistemology and the scientific method to answer questions within social science. I am interested in the philosophy of psychology, methodology, and measurements.
Outside of research, my passions include exploring cultures (I am half Japanese and half Latina), traveling, film theory, ‘The Fool’ archetype, fringe populations, and anti-ableism.
In my former life, I was an early employee of the crypto/fintech space at Ripple and Affirm. I am a graduate of UC Berkeley and can be found tinkering with LLMs, cycling, or on campus at the Gopnik Lab, D-Lab, Jacobs Institute, and the EECS department.
