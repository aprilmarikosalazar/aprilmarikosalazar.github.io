About

My background in Psychology (with an emphasis in Developmental Cognition) has led me to explore the causal pathways of cognition and decision-making in multiple real-world contexts. Guiding my work is a quasi-obsession with epistemology and the scientific method to answer questions within social science. I am deeply interested in the philosophy of psychology, methodology, and measurements. When consolidating literature, I am compelled to aggregate seemingly identical terminology often holding different meanings within each body of work. If common language was part of our field's social contract, we might be better able to locate the precise holes of our outstanding investigations.
My thoughts outside of research lie in numerous passions, such as exploring cultures (I am half Japanese and half Latina), traveling, film theory, ‘The Fool’ archetype, fringe populations, and anti-ableism.
In my former life, I was an early employee of the crypto/fintech space at Ripple and Affirm. I also have experience in project management. I am a graduate of UC Berkeley and former research assistant for the Gopnik Lab, CEGA, D-Lab, and the EECS department.
